Five Things to do for your Personal Finance during Covid-19 Outbreak

The COVID-19 pandemic has some lessons for all of us

Deepesh Raghaw

  • Mar 26
  • 5 min read

You might have sought answers to questions like “How can personal finance help you?” “How to learn personal finance?” “What is personal financial planning?” or “Why personal finance is important?”

These questions now have new answers, as we are in the middle of a coronavirus pandemic.

What has happened when you have suddenly stopped going to the office?

You saved more money, comforted yourself by buying groceries, got a moratorium on your EMI. You must have even faced salary cuts, saw the stock markets plunging, observed the interest rates falling and much more.

But, did you know that the personal finance decisions you make right now, while in quarantine, are going to affect your life once you start going to your office again

Here are the top five things you should be doing to manage your finances:

1) Avoid excess buying

You might have gone to a grocery store and would have panicked looking at empty shelves. Does your grocery app say that it can’t deliver the things you need the most? Is your regular vegetable vendor asking you to pay a premium price for your daily share of veggies? This recent piece by The Financial Times, London, will offer you a new perspective.

Soon those empty shelves in the grocery stores will be full. Like you, many others would have panicked and stock-piled the groceries. But in the coming weeks, the demand will steeply decline. And, we all know what happens when demand falls, the price goes down.

The vegetables and fruits, due to their perishable nature, would become inexpensive automatically. Moreover, the excess veggies that you had purchased will be wasted. Why spend on something you might have to throw later.

2) Revisit your spends

Many of you are saving in your travel time. You can spare this time for studying your spending patterns.

You might have used mobile apps for making payments. You can just visit your transaction history and make a note of the things that you have been lavishly spending your hard-earned money on.

You can classify your spending into different categories, such as food, clothes, bills, and more. It will help you manage excess spending after you’re out of quarantine.

3) Make an investment goal

The markets are bearish, and you might be hearing about the various opportunities to enter the market. Well, there is numerous information available online on “how to invest” and “where to invest”. With the help of an accomplished financial advisor, you can put your goals in order.

Think about the credit voucher you got because your airline tickets got cancelled. You will have to travel somewhere within a year to use that. Won’t it be better to start making travel goals for it? It would be important to start saving for your trip now!

4) Save for emergency

Has your company recently reduced your salary? It might seem challenging to think about saving for emergencies at this moment. However, with the underlying pressure of a soon to arrive recession, planning for emergencies is imperative. For this, the simple idea of saving before spending can help, as you are already spending less while staying indoors. A strong emergency corpus can help you stay afloat during uncertain times.

5) Consider paying your EMIs on time

You might have heard about the recent EMI moratorium announcement. My colleagues and I have been coming across plenty of questions about it. Here are a few quick points:

On availing the moratorium, either your EMI period or your amount will increase, depending on your bank, payment period and your remaining amount.

A heavy credit card bill after three months can look scary and challenging to deal with. Even if you opt for the moratorium, compound Interest will continue to apply on your loans, for the same period.
